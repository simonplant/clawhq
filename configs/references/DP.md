# DP.md — David Prince Trading Methodology

Core reference for DP/Inner Circle trading signals. Use this to interpret AM Call content, VTF alerts, and trade ideas. Equivalent to MANCINI.md for Mancini signals.

## Core Philosophy

- **Theme-driven**: Identify market themes, determine when they'll work, commit aggressively
- **Environment reader**: Assess market climate first, then select trades within it
- **Anticipate demand**: "Would everyone else want this?" not "Do I like this?"
- **Do less in tough markets**: Singles and doubles — don't swing for home runs
- **50% failure rate**: Enter with open mind, know half won't work. Management is what matters.
- **Details matter**: "Traders win big by attending to all little details"

## Morning Call Structure

DP's AM Call follows a consistent pattern:
1. **Futures and overnight** — directional bias, gap context
2. **Big themes** — macro drivers, war/trade/policy, sector rotation
3. **Analyst upgrades/downgrades** — price targets, rating changes
4. **Actionable trade ideas** — ranked by conviction, with levels
5. **Final thoughts** — outlook, risk factors, trading advice

## DP's Trading System

DP trades around a **core position model**. He's not scalping random tickers — he's building and managing positions in quality names at key levels.

### The Core Position Model

1. **Identify quality names** — strong earnings, market leadership, clear technical levels
2. **Buy quality in the hole** — wait for pullbacks to key MAs (8-day, 10-day, 21-day, 200-day)
3. **Build a core position** — enter at the MA, size based on conviction
4. **Trade around the core** — trim into strength, add on pullbacks, trail stops
5. **Swing high quality** — hold winners overnight ("gifting positions"), trail next day
6. **Go flat when uncertain** — cash is a position. Don't force trades.

### Conviction Scoring (for FOCUS phase)

When converting DP's AM Call into order-ready signals, score each idea:

**HIGH (0.70+) — Full size, LIMIT order**
- DP explicitly says he's in the trade or "definitely a buyer"
- Name + specific level + clear catalyst
- "My favorite name", "sizable position", "I'm aggressive here"
- Quality name at a key MA (21-day or 200-day) with earnings support
- Already building a core position

**MEDIUM (0.50-0.69) — Half size, LIMIT order at level**
- "I'd be interested at X", "it'd be buyable at X"
- Level identified but entry conditional ("if it pulls back")
- Relative strength play without specific catalyst
- "I'd probably add" — building on existing position

**LOW (0.30-0.49) — Watch only, no order unless confirmed**
- "Cute short", "not excited but possible"
- Level mentioned without conviction language
- Analyst-driven idea without DP's personal endorsement
- "Lazy long" — opportunistic, not planned

**AVOID (<0.30) — Skip**
- "Not right", "something's off", "off the table"
- Gap has killed the setup
- DP explicitly says don't chase

### Entry Patterns

- **Buy quality in the hole**: Quality name pulls back to key MA → buy the level, stop below
- **Relative strength**: Stocks stronger/weaker than the market for reversal-leadership trades
  - Look for RS stocks to go red-to-green
  - Weaker stocks drop faster than the market (short candidates)
  - Stronger stocks rise faster than the market (long candidates)
- **Post-earnings buy points**: Pro-gap → day-after range establishes → enter when price breaks day-after high
- **Buy-the-dip on events**: Ignore "sell-the-news" — shorts are myopic. Set up for the bounce.
- **Divergence for shorts**: Index divergences signal breakdowns (e.g., semis diverging from market)
- **Retests signal weakness**: Repeated retests without bounce → flush coming

### Key Patterns to Watch
- **Short squeeze awareness**: After down week + bounce, avoid shorting — may squeeze to 21-day MA
- **Special levels don't last**: Support/resistance loses significance with repeated tests — "they keep returning until it breaks"
- **Contrarian on expectations**: If everyone expects a move (e.g., lock-up expiration short), it may not happen
- **Earnings season caution**: Harder when stocks near highs — risk priced in

## Risk & Money Management

- **1% Rule**: Max risk per trade = 1% of capital
- **3-4 positions max**: Current market guidance
- **Take profits quickly**: Lower targets, "look for less"
- **Risk priority > entry price**: "Traders fixated on precise levels miss trades"
- **Management responsibilities**: Stops, exit targets, trims, position sizes, key levels, hedges
- **Go to cash when needed**: Don't force trades

### Position Sizing (Mike Bellafiore / SMB Capital influence)
1. Quality of the name
2. Quality of the idea
3. Risk/reward (swing vs lotto)
4. Focus on "special ideas" (high energy/excitement)
5. Planned trade vs intraday scalp
