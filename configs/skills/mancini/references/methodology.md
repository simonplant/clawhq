# Mancini Methodology Reference

## The Golden Rule (90/10)
- 10% of sessions are Mode 1 (linear trend). 90% are Mode 2 (chop/trap).
- Default assumption: Mode 2 until proven otherwise.

## The Cycle
Elevator Down → Failed Breakdown (FB) → Short Squeeze.
Every point of red = 0.5-1.0 points of green in the squeeze.
Uptrend squeezes: full retracement + expansion. Downtrend squeezes: violent but lower highs.

## Failed Breakdown (FB) Protocol

### Significant Low Criteria (what qualifies for FB)
- Prior Day's Low
- High-Magnitude Low (20+ pt move away)
- Support Shelf (3+ tests)

### Acceptance Types (confirmation before entry)
- **Type 1 — Supply Exhaustion:** Price recovers level, backtests from below, sells off. Sell-off fails to move away, price returns. Supply proven exhausted.
- **Type 2 — Selling Failure:** Price at/above recovered level, selling attempt fails to drive lower. "Dog and stick" — price keeps returning to the level.
- **Non-Acceptance:** For shallow flushes (<10 pts) or high-vol rips. Recover the low, move 5 pts above, hold several minutes.

### When to use which acceptance type
- Moderate flush with room to backtest → Type 1 or 2
- Deep/violent flush ("free fall", "knife") → Non-Acceptance (no room for backtest pattern)
- Fast rip recovery → Non-Acceptance

### The 5-Point Danger Zone
0-5 pts above recovered low = negative expectancy without confirmed acceptance. This is where retail FOMO provides liquidity for the real move.

### Double/Triple-Dip FBDs
Price can trigger FB, rally, lose the low again. Second/third recovery = higher conviction.

### Stop Placement
Several points below the absolute lowest low of the entire flush structure (the wick).

## Level-to-Level Management (75/15/10)
1. T1 — 75% off at first resistance
2. Move stop to several pts under break-even. **Never let green go red.**
3. T2 — 15% off at second target
4. Runner — 10% trails for Mode 1 days

## Level Hierarchy & Market Control
- **Bears in control:** Price below failed major shelf. Rallies are "backtest shorts."
- **Bulls in control:** Price recovered and holds above major shelf.

## Breakdown Trades
- 80% of level breakdowns in ES fail → FB reversals. Core methodology avoids shorts on breakdowns.
- If attempted: wait for bounce at level, if bounce fails to recover, short beneath bounce low.

## Profit Protection Mode
1. After a win, hold runner, take no other trades.
2. Second trade only if runner stops out AND new pre-planned setup triggers.
3. Subsequent risk from first trade's profits only.

## Discipline
- 1-2 trades max per day.
- Two losses = quit (kill switch).
- "15 minutes of work" once setup triggers.

## Trading Windows
- **7:30-8:30 AM EST:** Prime window. Overnight FBDs align with NY open.
- **11:00 AM-2:00 PM EST:** Avoid. Mid-day chop noise.
- **After 3:00 PM EST:** Secondary window. Late-day vol, final trend reclaims.
